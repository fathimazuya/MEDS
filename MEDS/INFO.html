FEVER

Fever is the temporary increase in the body's temperature in response to a disease or illness. A child has a fever when the temperature is at or above one of these levels: 100.4°F (38°C) measured in the bottom (rectally) 99.5°F (37.5°C) measured in the mouth (orally) 99°F (37.2°C) measured under the arm (axillary)


SYMPTOMS


Sweating, Chills and shivering, Headache, Muscle aches, Loss of appetite,etc

URINARY TRACT INFECTION

Bladder infections, also called urinary tract infections or UTIs, occur when ­bacteria build up in the urinary tract. A UTI can be found in children from infancy through the teen years and into adulthood. Symptoms of a UTI include pain or burning during urination, the need to urinate frequently or urgently, bedwetting or accidents by a child who knows to use the toilet, abdominal pain, or side or back pain.


SYMPTOMS


a need to pee more often than usual,
pain or discomfort when peeing,
sudden urges to pee,
feeling as though you're unable to empty your bladder fully,
pain low down in your tummy,etc
